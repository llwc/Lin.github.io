HW2

2.9 b

Since we know that
$$P(\cup_{i=1}^n A_i)=\sum_{k=1}^nP(A_k\cap A_{k-1}^C\cap \cdots\cap A_1^C)$$
Then we know that
$$P(A_k\cap A_{k-1}^C\cap \cdots\cap A_1^C)=P(A_k)-P(A_k\cap (\cap_{i=1}^{k-1}A_i^C)^C)=P(A_k)-P(A_k)\cap(\cup_{i=1}^{k-1}A_i)$$
$$=P(A_k)-P(\cup_{i=1}^{k-1}(A_k\cap A_i))$$ From last problem, we have
that
$$P(\cup_{i=1}^n A_i)\geq \sum_{i=1}^n P(A_i)-\sum_{i<j}P(A_i\cap A_j)$$
Then we apply to $P(\cup_{i=1}^{k-1}(A_k\cap A_i))$ get
$$P(\cup_{i=1}^{k-1}(A_k\cap A_i))\geq \sum_{i=1}^{k-1}P(A_k\cap A_i)-\sum_{i<j}P((A_k\cap A_i)\cap(A_k\cap A_j))=\sum_{i=1}^{k-1}P(A_k\cap A_i)-\sum_{i<j}P(A_k\cap A_i\cap A_j)$$
Then we get
$$P(\cup_{i=1}^n A_i)=\sum_{k=1}^nP(A_k\cap A_{k-1}^C\cap \cdots\cap A_1^C)=\sum_{i=1}^n(P(A_k)-P(\cup_{i=1}^{k-1}(A_k\cap A_i)))$$
$$\leq \sum_{k=1}^n(P(A_k)-(\sum_{i=1}^{k-1}P(A_k\cap A_i)-\sum_{i<j}P(A_k\cap A_i\cap A_j)))=\sum_{i=1}^nP(A_i)-\sum_{i<j}P(A_i\cap A_j)+\sum_{i<j<k}P(A_i\cap A_j\cap A_k)$$
Then we done.
